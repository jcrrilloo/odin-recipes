Classic Fluffy Pancakes

This recipe uses basic ingredients you likely already have in your kitchen. The key to fluffy pancakes is not to overmix the batter!

Yields

  • About 8-10 pancakes

    • Prep Time

  • 10 Minutes

    • Cook time:

  • 10-15m minutes

    • Ingredients

    • 1 ½ cups (190 g) all-purpose flour
    • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
    • 2 teaspoons baking powder
    • ½ teaspoon salt
    • 1 ¼ cups (295 ml) milk (any kind works: whole, 2%, or even almond milk)
    • 1 large egg
    • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted and slightly cooled
    • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract (optional, but recommended for flavor)
    • Extra butter or oil for the pan

    Instructions

    1. Combine Dry Ingredients: In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt. This helps to distribute the leavening agents evenly.
    2. Combine Wet Ingredients: In a separate medium bowl, whisk together the milk and the egg. Then, slowly whisk in the melted butter and vanilla extract (if using).
    3. Mix Batter: Pour the wet ingredients into the bowl with the dry ingredients. Gently mix with a whisk or spatula until everything is just combined. Do not overmix! A few lumps in the batter are perfectly fine and are the secret to tender pancakes.
    4. Rest the Batter (Optional but Recommended): Let the batter rest for 5-10 minutes while you heat your pan. This allows the gluten to relax and the baking powder to start working, resulting in a fluffier pancake.
    5. Heat the Pan: Place a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat. Add a small amount of butter or oil to coat the surface. You'll know the pan is ready when a drop of water sizzles and evaporates quickly.
    6. Cook the Pancakes: Pour about ¼ cup of batter onto the hot pan for each pancake. Cook for 2-3 minutes on the first side. You'll know it's time to flip when bubbles start to form and pop on the surface and the edges look dry.
    7. Flip and Finish: Flip the pancakes carefully with a thin spatula and cook for another 1-2 minutes on the other side, until golden brown.
    8. Serve and Enjoy: Serve the pancakes immediately while hot, with your favorite toppings like butter, maple syrup, fresh fruit, or whipped cream.

    Pro-Tip: To keep the first batch of pancakes warm while you cook the rest, place them on a baking sheet in an oven preheated to 200°F (95°C).
